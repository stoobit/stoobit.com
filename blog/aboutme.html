Hi, I'm Till!

Hello, I'm Till, a 16-year-old student from Germany and developer of the note-taking app Productivity Pro. When I got my first MacBook at the end of 2020, I discovered Apple's Swift Playgrounds app in the AppStore after a while. Although I had previously been very interested in technology, it was this app that first introduced me to programming. After understanding the basics of programming, I learned about game development and Unity, but only superficially.

When I got to 9th grade, some of my classmates started replacing their folders or notebooks with iPads. I wanted to do that too, but since I didn't have enough money to buy an iPad, I had to wait until May (the school year in Germany starts in September). Then around January 2022 I had an idea – Why not write the note-taking app for school yourself? On the one hand I was up for the challenge and on the other hand I didn't like a few things I had seen on my friends' note-taking apps. Since I had started with Swift, the basis for this project was already there. However, I had no clue about user interface frameworks like SwiftUI and UIKit, so I spent some time learning the basics first. Then, a month later, in February, I created the Xcode project for my app.

The first challenge was to learn how to create document-based apps in SwiftUI and in the beginning I had some difficulties. But after I managed that, I programmed that you can choose templates and create pages, I added different shapes and made text fields. Since I'm a big fan of Markdown, the text fields in Productivity Pro are also working with Markdown, although there are probably some people who don't like it. The markup feature of Productivity Pro is based on Apple's PencilKit, because I really like the design of the toolpicker and wanted to have it in my app. Then I added the ability to import images & PDFs and scan documents and in the process I implemented an overview for the pages.

But during the whole development there was a problem that constantly accompanied me. Since you should be able to zoom and scroll into the notes, I had to implement a scroll view. And since the scroll view in SwiftUI was very limited, I had to resort to the UIScrollView from UIKit. And although I had already successfully done this with both the PencilKit and the possibility of scanning documents, I still had many difficulties with the ScrollView, so that I despaired many times and it took a total of more than four months until the result was satisfactory.

After almost a year and a half, on Friday, July 14, 2023, the time had finally come. Productivity Pro appeared on the AppStore, the first coding project I ever fully completed. And even if it was sometimes very exhausting and I often didn't know what to do, most of the time it was a lot of fun developing Productivity Pro. You can download the app for €5.99 or $4.99 (US) from the AppStore and I really appreciate every purchase. Not just because it makes money, but above all because I can see that my app is helping people and taking them further.
